Why I Switched My DNS to Cloudflare (and You Should Too)

Every DNS query your machine makes is a question shouted into a room. Whoever answers owns a piece of your browsing map.

For months my system was using Quad9 (9.9.9.9) as primary with Google (8.8.8.8) and Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) as fallbacks — and the per-link DNS was handed out by my Singtel ONT at 192.168.1.254, which forwards to Singtel's own resolvers.

I switched to Cloudflare. Here's why, and what changed.

--

Step 1: What DNS Was I Actually Using?

$ resolvectl status
Global:
  Current DNS Server: 9.9.9.9#dns.quad9.net
  Fallback DNS: 9.9.9.9, 1.1.1.1, 8.8.8.8

Link 2 (wlan0):
  Current DNS Server: 192.168.1.254
  DNS Servers: 192.168.1.254

The ONT (192.168.1.254) was the per-link resolver for the WiFi connection. That means every DNS query from this machine first went to the Singtel-issued ZTE ONT, which forwarded to Singtel's upstream DNS resolvers — likely running on infrastructure shared with their caching infrastructure.

This matters because:

  1. Your ISP logs DNS queries. In Singapore, the IMDA mandates that ISPs retain certain communication data. DNS logs are part of that. Your ISP knows every domain you visit unless you use an external encrypted resolver.
  2. The ONT's cache is tiny. A consumer ONT has a DNS cache measured in kilobytes. Every unique domain is a cache miss and an upstream query.
  3. Default resolvers are slow. Singtel's DNS infrastructure is designed for reliability, not speed. It's adequate, but it's not optimised.

Step 2: The Speed Test

I benchmarked the four resolvers from this Singtel Fibre connection (AS9506, Ulu Bedok):

$ hyperfine -w 3 \
  'dig +short @192.168.1.254 google.com' \
  'dig +short @8.8.8.8 google.com' \
  'dig +short @9.9.9.9 google.com' \
  'dig +short @1.1.1.1 google.com'

Benchmark 1: @192.168.1.254 (Singtel ONT)
  Time (mean ± σ):     28.2 ms ± 3.1 ms

Benchmark 2: @8.8.8.8 (Google)
  Time (mean ± σ):     4.8 ms ± 0.9 ms

Benchmark 3: @9.9.9.9 (Quad9)
  Time (mean ± σ):     5.7 ms ± 1.2 ms

Benchmark 4: @1.1.1.1 (Cloudflare)
  Time (mean ± σ):     2.1 ms ± 0.6 ms

Cloudflare: 2ms. That's essentially the same data centre. Cloudflare has a Singapore edge node at Equinix SG — my packet never leaves the island.

Google and Quad9 are also fast (5ms) — both have Singapore PoPs. The Singtel ONT is the clear loser at 28ms — almost certainly a cache miss forwarded to an upstream ISP resolver.

Step 3: The Privacy Argument

Every time you resolve a domain, you tell the resolver what you're doing:

ResolverLogging PolicyAudit
Singtel ONT (default)Retained per IMDA regulationsKnows everything
Google 8.8.8.8Logs anonymised after 24-48hThird-party
Quad9 9.9.9.9No logging, Swiss-basedIndependently audited
Cloudflare 1.1.1.1No logs, logs deleted within 24hAudited by KPMG

The difference between Singtel's default and Cloudflare:

  • Singtel can correlate your DNS queries with your public IP (116.15.188.9), your ONT's serial number, and your billing address
  • Cloudflare sees your queries but doesn't log them after 24 hours and has no way to tie them to your Singtel account

Cloudflare also supports DNS-over-HTTPS (DoH) and DNS-over-TLS (DoT) natively, meaning your DNS queries are encrypted in transit — no eavesdropping on the local network or at the ONT level.

Step 5: The Result

$ resolvectl query google.com
google.com: 142.250.196.78                    -- link: wlan0
             2404:6800:4009:82f::200e
[...]
Cache hit? yes

Every DNS query now resolves in ~2ms instead of ~28ms. The cache hit rate on Cloudflare's resolver is higher because Cloudflare sees queries from millions of users across Singapore and can serve cached responses from the same edge node.

In practice, this means:

  • Page load times: marginal improvement (DNS is rarely the bottleneck)
  • Privacy: significant — my ISP no longer has a DNS-level map of my browsing
  • Reliability: Cloudflare's resolver has 100% uptime SLA and anycast failover
  • Blocked domains: none (Cloudflare does not censor; 1.1.1.2/1.1.1.3 versions add malware blocking if desired)

What About DNS-over-HTTPS?

Cloudflare resolver supports DoH at https://cloudflare-dns.com/dns-query. Modern browsers (Firefox, Chrome) can be configured to use DoH directly, bypassing the system resolver entirely. This provides:

  • Encrypted queries: your network admin / ISP can't see which domains you're resolving
  • No local cache: bypasses the ONT's tiny cache
  • OS independence: works on any network, even strict hotel WiFi
# curl example using DoH
$ curl -s 'https://cloudflare-dns.com/dns-query?name=google.com&type=A' \
  -H 'accept: application/dns-json' | jq .Answer

Should You Switch?

If you ...Switch to Cloudflare
Want faster DNS✅ 2ms vs 28ms from Singtel ONT
Don't want Singtel logging your browsing✅ No query logging after 24h
Use encrypted DNS (DoH/DoT)✅ Built-in support
Need malware blockingUse 1.1.1.2 (blocks malware) or 1.1.1.3 (blocks malware + adult content)
Are paranoid about CloudflareStay on Quad9 (Swiss-based, independently audited)

I switched for two reasons: the 2ms resolver latency (I like fast things) and keeping my DNS map off Singtel's logging infrastructure (I like private things). The ~26ms per-query improvement adds up when you consider that a typical web page triggers 50-100 DNS lookups.

DNS benchmarks run from a Singtel Fibre Broadband connection (AS9506) in Ulu Bedok, Singapore. Times are the mean of 10 warm runs after 3 warmup iterations. All resolvers were available and responding during the test window.
