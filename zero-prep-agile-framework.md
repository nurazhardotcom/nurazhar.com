Agile follows a predictable pattern. This framework maps their jargon onto systems-thinking concepts — useful for translating between the two vocabularies.

---

## 1. The Core Philosophy (The "Why")

| Their Jargon | Your Translation |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Embracing change over rigid planning | **Collapse entropy** by delivering small chunks instead of planning a massive fragile system |
| Iterative value delivery | Ship functioning subsystems every cycle |
| Continuous feedback loops | Each iteration is a sensor reading. Use it to correct course. |

**One-liner:** They talk about agility. You talk about entropy reduction. Same thing, better frame.

---

## 2. The Mechanics (The Scrum Loop)

Memorize this exact flow:

1. **Product Backlog** — The master list of raw requirements, prioritized by business value. An entropy-ordered queue.
2. **Sprint Planning** — Select a high-priority chunk from the backlog for a fixed 2-week window. Fixed-capacity entropy batch.
3. **Daily Standup** — 15-minute sync. Identify roadblocks (impediments). Maintain zero-entropy alignment.
4. **Sprint Review & Retrospective** — Demo the working increment to stakeholders. Then optimize the team's internal execution protocol for the next cycle.

---

## 3. Handling Impediments (The Pivot)

If they ask how you handle a crisis, delayed dependency, or changing requirement — the answer is **always**:

> *"We log it as an impediment, discuss it immediately in the daily standup, pivot the Sprint backlog if necessary to preserve velocity, and ensure stakeholder transparency."*

That sentence covers the SM role, empiricism, backlog management, and communication. All in one breath.
