Title: Your Scrum Master Is Not Your Project Manager
Date: 2026-07-01
Tags: scrum, scrum-master, project-manager, agile, leadership
Description: Scrum Master is a servant-leader who coaches and removes blockers. Project Manager is a command-and-control director. They are fundamentally different roles.

---

![Your Scrum Master Is Not Your Project Manager](scrum-master-vs-pm.svg)

Scrum has **no Project Manager role**. The Scrum Master exists because traditional command-and-control management kills self-managing teams. One role is a **servant-leader**; the other is a **command-and-control director**.

---

## Scrum Master: Servant-Leader

The SM serves the team. They **coach**, **facilitate**, and **remove blockers** — but they never tell anyone what to do.

| Responsibility | What It Actually Means |
|---------------|----------------------|
| **Coach** | Teaches Scrum, self-management, and cross-functionality |
| **Facilitator** | Runs Sprint events so they're productive and timeboxed |
| **Blocker Remover** | Shields the team from interruptions; clears organizational impediments |
| **Process Owner** | Ensures the Scrum framework is followed correctly |

**Authority:** Zero formal authority. The SM cannot hire, fire, assign tasks, or override team decisions. Their power is **influence through trust**.

> "The Scrum Master is a true leader who serves the Scrum Team and the larger organization." — Scrum Guide 2020

---

## Project Manager: Command & Control

The PM owns the outcome. They **direct**, **assign**, and **report** — everything flows through them.

| Responsibility | What It Actually Means |
|---------------|----------------------|
| **Director** | Assigns tasks to individuals and tells them how to do it |
| **Budget Owner** | Manages the budget, timeline, and resource allocation |
| **Risk Manager** | Identifies and mitigates risks to the project plan |
| **Stakeholder Reporter** | Communicates status upward and keeps execs informed |

**Authority:** Full formal authority. The PM can hire, fire, make scope decisions, and overrule team estimates.

---

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Dimension | Scrum Master | Project Manager |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Leadership style** | Servant-leader | Command & control |
| **Decision-maker** | The Developers (self-managing) | The PM (top-down) |
| **Task assignment** | None — team pulls work | Assigns tasks to individuals |
| **Budget** | Not involved | Owns it |
| **Timeline** | Scrum events are fixed; delivery is empirical | Gantt charts, deadlines, milestones |
| **Authority** | Influence only | Formal authority |
| **Team asks** | "How can I help?" | "Report your status" |
| **Primary tool** | Coaching questions | Status reports, risk registers |

---

## The Hard Truth

A Scrum Master can't fix a toxic PM-driven culture by holding retrospectives. And a PM can't drive agile transformation by emailing Gantt charts. These are **opposite operating systems**.

Your Scrum Master is not your Project Manager. If you're being **assigned work** and **asking permission**, you have a PM wearing an SM hat — and that's a problem.

---

*Scrum Master serves the team. Project Manager serves the plan. Know which one you need.*
